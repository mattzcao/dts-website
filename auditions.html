AUDITION FOR US

We are currently holding Fall 2020 auditions for ALL VOICE PARTS. All Cal students are invited to audition -- no prior experience necessary!

Please read the following guidelines carefully, since auditions will be in a very different format during the online semester.

Preliminary auditions will be in the form of a video submission, which can be submitted by Friday, September 11, 11:59pm. The video should consist of:

1. A brief introduction: name, year, major, and anything else you'd like to include to introduce yourself!

2. Warmups: Download the two following links and sing along to the warmups, going as high and as low as you feel comfortable singing. For the ascending warmups, use the syllable "vee" going up and "vah" going down. For descending warmups, use the syllable "yah" for each note going down. It is okay if you need to skip one or two sequences to find your note.

3. Solos: Please prepare TWO short solos (a verse and a chorus) for your audition. We prefer them to be contrasting so you can show off more versatility with your voice, but feel free to choose any songs you feel confident singing.

Once you've finished recording your audition video, please call it "FirstName-LastName-AUDITION" and email it to cal.drawntoscale@gmail.com with the subject "FA20 AUDITION". Lastly, fill out the Google Form and you're all set.

We will reach out to you through email Saturday afternoon, September 12th, with results from your audition, and how to move on to the next step if you are called back!

Since this is a new process for all of us, don't hesitate to email us at cal.drawntoscale@gmail.com with any questions or confusions about the audition process. Thanks for your interest in DtS!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q. How do auditions work?

A. Our audition process comprises a round of preliminary auditions with tests of vocal range, tonal memory, sight singing, and solo ability, and a second round of callbacks. Preliminary auditions last 15-20 minutes. You will be notified a few hours to a few days after your preliminary audition about whether you have made it through to callbacks. Callbacks can take up to the entire day depending upon how many auditionees are called back. However, we do make accommodations for those with time constraints.

Q. What do I need to do to prepare for a preliminary audition?

A. We strongly recommend that auditionees warmup prior to the preliminary audition, as we will be testing your upper and lower range, and ability to follow our warmup exercise. We expect auditionees to prepare a solo piece between 1-2 minutes long – about the length of a verse and chorus of a pop song.

Q. But I can’t sight read! What should I do?

A. Don't worry! We want to see how quickly you can learn music, and one of those methods is sight singing. Some people learn better by reading music, while others learn by ear. Either is fine.

Q. What should I sing for my audition?

A. Sing a part of a song that showcases your voice well and you enjoy singing. Also, please make sure the song is in English!

Q. But I’m nervous!

A. We know. We understand that the audition process can be a nerve-wracking one. We’ve been there and were probably nervous trainwrecks at our own auditions, so we understand. We’ll try to get you to relax so you can perform to the best of your abilities.

Q. How do callbacks work?

A. If you are given a callback, congratulations! You will be given 1-2 short pieces to learn. The purpose of this is to see how well your voice is able to blend with the current group. You will also be asked to perform your solo piece again, along with some of the exercises from the preliminary audition individually in front of all of the members of the current group.

Q. What are the time commitments involved?

A. Our rehearsals are Sundays and Wednesdays 6-9 pm, apart from weekly one-hour sectionals. We also have weekly Sproul performances on Friday at noon, with warmups beginning at 11 am. We also have other events during the semester as well as a concert near the end of each semester. We expect members to be able to make all rehearsals and Sproul performances, and a number of other events. Being a member of an a cappella group could be a significant time commitment, but we enjoy every moment spent together as a performance group!

Q. Should I audition again if I have auditioned previously?

A. Definitely! Selections at the preliminary and the callback levels are always very difficult for us due to our limited time and the number of voices per part we need changes from semester to semester. We have seen many wonderful singers who showed great promise and simply needed a little bit more experience. Some of our members have also auditioned multiple times before getting into the group, so don’t be afraid to re-audition.
